Sign Language Transcriber

Doing communcation the right way

About

Humans are used to processing and understanding audio-visual information. This is why video conferencing is so appealing and video chat applications have gained popularity in the past years. However, with these apps, people who are hard of hearing have to use text to communicate during video calls. Our application, Sign Language Transcriber, will be able to convert ASL (American Sign Language) alphabets into text and provide it as closed captions to ensure that video calls are effectively accessible to all people.

Our Features

Our approach to this problem was to use computer vision and deep learning algorithms to have the computer classify ASL as text during video call. With our application - the Sign Language Transcriber, people will be able to communicate with their peers as the computer will provide real-time captioning for ASL.

Design

The system was first trained on Images of people using ASL to create the deep learning model. This model was then used by the application to convert ASL in a video call to closed captions for the receiver to read

Technology Stack

We decided to simplify our website design to accommodate the integration of the deep learning aspects of the transcriber. The main echnologies used include: Python, JavaScript, Jquery, Kaggle, webpack, bootstrap, AWS-Elastic-beanstalk

Improvements

Hope to incorportate more advance technologies for the application such as react or vue, and allow for the fast and active reading of full words rather than just letters
